Course Overview

Design Thinking Methodology

A semester-long journey through analytical rigor, systems thinking, and structured problem framing.

Pedagogical Structure

The semester is organised around three progressive layers of engagement:

1 Context Immersion

Students begin with observation, stakeholder mapping, secondary research, and field inquiry. They are trained to distinguish symptoms from root causes and to recognise systemic relationships within real-world situations. Emphasis is placed on evidence gathering rather than premature solutioning.

2 Problem Framing & Reframing

A significant portion of the course is devoted to defining the problem statement. Students generate multiple framings of the same situation and evaluate how each framing alters the design direction. This stage develops intellectual flexibility and the ability to handle complexity without oversimplification.

3 Ideation & Concept Structuring

Only after rigorous analysis do students move into ideation. Concepts are evaluated not just for novelty, but for feasibility, scalability, ethical implications, and alignment with contextual constraints. Students are required to justify decisions using structured reasoning.

While prototyping is introduced when relevant, the course does not prioritise rapid artefact production. In most cases, projects culminate in clearly articulated design proposals supported by research, diagrams, decision matrices, and visual communication. The objective is conceptual robustness rather than superficial completeness.

What Students Develop

By the end of the semester, students demonstrate:

  • The ability to engage with ambiguity without rushing to closure
  • Structured problem decomposition and systems thinking
  • Logical argumentation for design decisions
  • Awareness of social, technical, and ethical constraints
  • Confidence in articulating complex ideas clearly

Why This Matters

In professional practice, the most consequential design decisions are often made before anything is built. The ability to frame problems correctly, interrogate assumptions, and reason through alternatives is what differentiates thoughtful practitioners from mere executors.

This gallery therefore presents not only proposed solutions, but the cognitive processes behind them. The work reflects disciplined thinking, contextual sensitivity, and structured decision-making developed over the course of the semester.
